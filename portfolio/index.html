Data challenge: OpenStreetMap

Looking at OpenStreetMap data for Shropshire I realised there was a lot of landuse mapped in the South West of the county, around Bishop’s Castle and Clun Forest. So I started thinking what I could do with that. The multipolygon layers also contained administrative boundaries, e.g. with admin_level = 10. Apparently those are civil parishes. So I decided to compare the ratio of woodland/forest to area in a selection of parishes.

Metamapping

The brief for this day way to “write about what you’ve done with the other maps, or document or collect together in some way.” I decided to set up an online portfolio using the Hugo static site generator. To make this possible, I challenged myself to use entirely open or creative commons data so that I could share the end results, and tried to ensure that I acknowledged all data I used.
